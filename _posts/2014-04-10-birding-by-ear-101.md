<div class="paragraph" style="text-align:center;">We had a great presentation on April 9 by Donna Finnegan, helping us get a sense of "Birding by Ear." &nbsp;There was a great crowd and everyone enjoyed her presentation helping us recognize more of what surrounds us every day.<br /><br />One of the big themes of her presentation was the use of mnemonics and phonetics to help get a handle on recognizing birds by their songs. &nbsp;She had a list with her that we'll post on the site as soon as we can. &nbsp;In the meantime, there are several places on the Internet where you can find this kind of list. &nbsp;A nice one I found today comes from the <a href="http://fsc.fernbank.edu/Birding/mnemonics.htm" target="_blank" title="">Fernbank Science Center in Atlanta</a>.<br /><br />There are also several guides and apps that help with song ID (though they all have the problem of starting with knowing the bird and then hearing the song. . . we need something that lets us record a song and have the app tell us what we're hearing. . .) We'll be adding more posts about these programs and how they work for us.<br /><br />Wendy also reminded us that some folks will be meeting at Boxerwood on Saturday the 12th at 8 am to practice - everyone is welcome.
